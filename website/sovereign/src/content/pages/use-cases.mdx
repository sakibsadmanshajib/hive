## The shared problem

For regulated work, sensitive data cannot route through an external AI endpoint.
For everyone else, per-token US billing makes useful AI too expensive to run at
the volume the work actually needs. Hive answers both: open-weight models on your
own hardware for regulated work, and Hive Cloud at regional pricing for teams
that want the economics without running their own box.

## Law

Pain: client matter is privileged. Sending a brief, a contract, or a discovery
set to a US-owned AI endpoint puts that material under a foreign jurisdiction and
into a third party's logs, where it can be compelled or pulled into discovery.

Hive capability: open model weights run on your own hardware, with retrieval over
your matter files happening inside your network. Every inference executes locally.

Exposure removed: no prompt, no response, no document leaves your network
boundary, so there is nothing in an external provider's hands to compel or
disclose. Whether this satisfies your obligations is a determination for your own
counsel; this is general information, not legal advice.

## Finance

Pain: proprietary signals, credit models, and client positions are the firm's
edge. Routing them through an external endpoint hands your most valuable data to
a third party and creates an obligation question under frameworks such as GLBA
and the FTC Safeguards Rule.

Hive capability: on-prem inference with a private retrieval layer over your own
documents and data. The API is OpenAI-compatible, so the tools your desk already
uses point at Hive with a base-url change.

Exposure removed: model weights and every inference stay on your hardware, so
proprietary data never leaves the building. Regulatory fit is a determination for
your compliance team and counsel; this is general information, not advice.

## Healthcare

Pain: patient data and clinical notes are the most sensitive records you hold.
An external AI endpoint means protected health information crosses your boundary
into a third party's systems.

Hive capability: open-weight models run on your own hardware, air-gap capable for
the most restricted environments. No external API is ever called.

Exposure removed: no PHI leaves the building. Whether this architecture meets your
obligations under HIPAA or any other framework is a determination your team must
make with qualified counsel; this is general information, not advice.

## Small and growing teams

Pain: useful AI is priced by the token, billed in US dollars, and the bill climbs
with every request. At real working volume the per-token model prices the work
out before it pays off.

Hive capability: Hive Cloud runs the same open-weight models behind the same
OpenAI-compatible API, at regional pricing that is a fraction of the US rate, on
a plan you can predict.

Outcome: you get capable AI your team can actually afford to use every day, with
no change to the code you already wrote against the OpenAI API.
